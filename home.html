WELCOME TO PUSHING LIMITS

This website is a Documentary pitch, intended to spark interest in potential producers, networks and other partners such as sponsors etc.

CREATOR: Caleb Dibue Mutombo BACKGROUND & KEY CREDITS Caleb Dibwe Mutombo a physically challenged bodybuilder. Born on 26-06- 1997 in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

My story

“My family bought me to South Africa when I was only 2 years old, in search of medical treatment for my brother Kalala and I. Financial disputes transpired in the family and things go south... Fast forward I started working out at the age of 12 and I became serious resources bodybuilding at the age of 16 when, I had a discussion with one of my teachers resources bodybuilding. She encouraged me to look it up, and I did. For three years I pushed my dream, with sheer discipline, when no one believed in me until one day I was picked up by the local newspaper.I found my 15min of fame and enjoyed it. I went on to win 3rd place in the 2016 Summer Super Bodies Bodybuilding competition and 1st place in the 2019 Sharemen Classic bodybuilding competition in. I now live in Cape Town South Africa.”

WHAT IS THE GOAL OF THE DOCUMENTARY FILM?

The purpose of the documentary film is to spark courage and inspire a relentless strong will in people, by engaging the emotions of the audience, I want them to feel inspired to never give up! It is in these recent trying times, with a lot of economic despair, climate change, and political division with many people still recovering from the losses of the pandemic. I think it’s time that people see something positive for a change. I want to be that beacon of light, that shines on the path of hope. We can achieve this by filming my three bodybuilding goals. With the push-up world record being the main goal and the sit-world record being the build-up to the main goal and the fitness course being a bonus goal.

You will belive it when you see it!

LOGLINE

A young physically challenged black man, must fly to the USA where he will complete his 3 yearly goals. To do this he must fight his fears and insecurities, by training hard in the gym and finding his passion for bodybuilding just one more time. All in the hopes that he may inspire the world to never give up and train with his bodybuilding hero Arnold Schwarzenegger. Here’s how… 1. Break the world record for the most sit-ups in one hour. (Build-up to meet Arnold 6 months training June-Feb) 2. Break the world record for the most push-ups in one Hours (Main goal. Meet Arnold goal in 12 months June-June next year) 3. Finish a fitness diploma. (Bonus 12 months June-June next year) I presume that it can all be shot within 1 year or a year and a half. So, I have set Annual and Biannual goals. Each goal has meaning as you will learn later on. I originally set one goal, but now I think it’s better to have a sub-goal creating a climax to the end goal and a bonus goal as something special for our viewers who finish the documantary.

SYNOPSIS

Pushing limits is a documentary that follows an inspiring young black physically challenged individual on his day-to-day journey, chasing his bodybuilding goals. You can think of it like a bucket list. The original idea was imitated when a proposal by a Guinness book of world record researcher, reached out to Caleb Dibue Mutombo, resources breaking some kind of world record. The main story takes place in LA California USA, while his history and end goal take place in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Johannesburg, and Cape Town South Africa. Caleb was born in Congo DRC but, grew up in JHB and now lives in Cape Town. (As of the time of this writing). The reason for this is that the majority of film funds, including the largest ones reside in the USA and some do not fund outside of the USA, especially government grants. In addition, with goals being set in the US. It makes media coverage much easier and so, does meeting with Arnold if he agrees! The training scenes will be shot in LA California, with more scenes such as interviews being shot in Cape Town, Johannesburg South Africa, and possibly the DRC, because most of the people that are relevant to the story live in the DRC, JHB, and or Cape Town. The world record-breaking event will happen in two places. 1. The NFL Superbowl in Las Vegas Nevada. February 2024 (a long shot, but worth trying) 2. The Arnold Classic South Africa. May or June 2024.
